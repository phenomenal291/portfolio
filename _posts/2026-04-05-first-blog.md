**First Blog**
11:22 am, warm Sunday April 5th.

Yesterday I finished uploading the project description and deployed my first portfolio on GitHub Pages. It was an interesting experience to publicly display some of my side projects in programming and physics simulation, and create something that expresses myself while practicing journaling.

Especially in the AI-fication era where external intelligence can replace human thinking and perform intellectual work better than us, I believe I should at least preserve human attributes and build a unique personality before it is too late. Therefore, I choose to write these lines myself, not through LLM assistants, even though I am not confident in English.

Because in school, many students including me use Gemini and ChatGPT for reports. I could say many reports and essays are now AI-generated. Ironically, teachers may also use AI to review and grade those essays. It becomes an AI loop, and whoever prompts better gets a better score.

So I am starting this blog page as a place where I can share side hobbies, lingering thoughts, and especially journaling. I believe journaling is a way to practice self-reflection, or simply self-talk. I am writing for anyone to read, but mostly for myself, the one writing it.

It helps me push stubborn feelings and opinions out of my mind for peace and relief, while I can slowly look back at myself and consider what is happening with me and what I am thinking.

Every day I consume a lot of information from social media without filters or limits, and it gets worse if I do not take action. Recently, I tried to stay away from social media, remove abundant information sources, and selectively choose what I want to study. Sometimes I fail and go back to doomscrolling and cheap dopamine, but I can be conscious about it and fix it after that. Though it has been rough, I can see myself becoming better every day.

For future plans, I am going to continue this blog frequently, pursue my pet projects, and survive the tough job market. I need an internship next year for graduation.

My next blog could be about my opinion on programming and its future from a sophomore's perspective.
